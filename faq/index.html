Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about using the iFocus platform.

🔒 Privacy

How do I change my password?

When you are logged in, go to Settings > Manage Privacy, and update your password there.

How do you ensure the protection of patient privacy?

We prioritize patient privacy in our digital health product. We implement several measures to protect sensitive information, including encryption of data transmission, secure storage practices, and strict access controls. We comply with all applicable privacy laws and regulations and continuously monitor and update our security protocols to mitigate risks and maintain the confidentiality of patient data.

💊 Medications

How do I add or update my medication information?

On the landing page, select the "Add new medication" button if you are a first-time user, or "Change medication" if you already have at least one medication recorded. Next, enter the details of your medications — please provide the name and strength as they appear on the bottle, as well as the number of pills you take and when you take them.

Watch this video for a visual explanation: youtube.com/watch?v=smuqRFeYZco

Can I add multiple medications, strengths, or schedules?

Yes! If your prescription includes multiple medications, click the "+" sign next to the medication name and enter the details of your second medication. Different strengths or times can be added by clicking the "+" sign next to the appropriate field.

Watch this video for a visual explanation: youtube.com/watch?v=smuqRFeYZco

What if I take the medication at a different time than usual? Can I still perform a session?

On the landing page, where your current medication is listed, click the time listed next to it to update the actual time taken.

Watch this video for a visual explanation: youtube.com/watch?v=smuqRFeYZco

📖 Reading Session

What data is collected and saved during a session when the camera is on?

We are recording only eye position and movements. We are not recording or saving any video or audio footage.

For more information: watch the video

I have multiple webcams. Which one should I use?

Use the camera which is best situated in front of you so we can get the best view of your face.

For more information: watch the video

I got an error message at the end of the session. What should I do?

If you got an error message saying: "The data we collected is not of high enough quality to generate a reliable result. Please ensure that you are in a well-lit space and try again."

A session may be rejected if the data collected is of poor quality, often due to lighting, not reading the full page, or other factors. Try performing another session. If this problem persists, please contact our support.

When should I perform a session?

Perform a session 1–3 hours after taking medication for medication peak effectiveness and most accurate results.

Can I use the app with glasses or contact lenses?

Yes! The eye-tracking works the same with or without corrective lenses.

📝 Self Reported Assessment

Why are you asking about my sleep, mood, and appetite?

These are among the most common side effects that play a big role in your medication satisfaction. We want to provide a comprehensive report including your self-assessment and effectiveness measurements. You can add more side effects in the free text box, and you will see all data you reported in the downloadable report.

📈 iFocus Measure Score

How many sessions should I complete to get an iFocus score?

We compare your reading with and without medication and personalize the score based on your results. Performing 2 sessions will give you a preliminary iFocus score. The more sessions you complete, the higher the iFocus accuracy score! You can perform 5 sessions for an iFocus super score.

For more information: watch the video

📋 Report

What is in the report and how do I get it?

In the report you will find the results of all the medications you tested with the iFocus Measure. You will see iFocus and self-reported scores, and the reported side effects for each medication tested. You will also get a list of all the sessions you performed and the correlated data for each session separately.

You can download a report and save it locally on your computer, or email it to yourself, and then share it with your physician.
